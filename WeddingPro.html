Haldi Ceremony

The haldi ceremony is a pre-wedding ritual in Indian weddings that involves covering the bride and groom in a turmeric paste called haldi. The ceremony is usually held a day or two before the wedding and is a significant event in almost every Indian regional wedding. The word haldi is the Hindi word for turmeric, which gives the paste its golden color. The ceremony symbolizes purity, good luck, and love

Mehndi Ceremony

Mehndi, otherwise known as henna, is a paste associated with positive spirits and good luck. Indian Wedding tradition calls for a Mehndi ceremony to be held the night before the wedding as a way of wishing the bride good health and prosperity as she makes her journey on to marriage.

pre Wedding

Pre-wedding ceremonies include engagement, which involves vagdana (betrothal) and Lagna-patra (written declaration), and Varyatra— the arrival of the groom's party at the bride's residence, often as a formal procession with dancing and music.
